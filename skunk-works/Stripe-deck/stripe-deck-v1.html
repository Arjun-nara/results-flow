Project 01 · Wealthsimple Cash · 2022

External Account Linking

Building trust and driving engagement — keeping cash flowing beyond the referral program

iOS & Android IC Designer 6 weeks A/B Test Fintech · Payments
Context

A referral program that worked too well

Wealthsimple Cash targeted 18–25 year olds as a better alternative to e-transfers — Canada's answer to Venmo or Cash App.

A $15–$25 referral bonus drove explosive campus growth. Some students earned close to $1,000 through referrals alone.

But once the money left their wallets, users reverted to e-transfers — or left the ecosystem entirely.

The Discovery

There was a hidden feature nobody knew about

Frequent senders would top up wallets and keep sending — but there was an untapped loop: sending directly from an external bank account. Almost nobody used it.

1 in 10
users knew they could send from a bank account
47%
of frequent senders already had a connected external account
~0
visible benefit shown to users for linking — the toggle went unnoticed
Why It Matters

Users who linked an account were meaningfully different

  • More likely to engage in frequent send activity
  • Less likely to exploit the referral program
  • More likely to fund WS Invest and Trade — the actual revenue drivers
  • Less likely to leave the Wealthsimple ecosystem entirely
  • More likely to have a higher average send amount — setting up a future bill pay feature
"If we could get more people linking accounts, we could get more engaged users and fewer bad actors."
Problem Framing

Four reasons the feature stayed invisible

  • Default source was always the WS Wallet — no prompt to switch
  • The toggle to change source was easy to miss
  • No visible benefit to linking a bank account
  • Users had trust concerns about linking a personal account

A tooltip band-aid had minimal effect. We needed something deeper.

Exploring the solution space

Final Design

From toggle to intentional selection

Replaced the toggle with a bottom sheet that forces an explicit source choice. Added an account-linking page addressing the trust and value gap.

Reflection

What I'd do differently

Push harder on the linking flow

The plateau near the end of the experiment suggests the education page wasn't enough. A fuller linking flow redesign was probably warranted.

Quantify the default bias risk earlier

I flagged it verbally — I should have put data behind it to make the tradeoff clearer for the PM and stakeholders.

Instrument trust signals

We relied on qualitative feedback for trust concerns. Better to have added in-app trust signals and measured them directly.

Plan for iteration from day one

Shipped, saw a plateau, then the window closed. Baking iteration cycles into the timeline upfront would have helped.

Project 02 · Bench · 2020–21

Pursuit Tracking

Helping construction companies understand and plan for their future workforce

Web · iOS · Android IC Designer 4 months Generative + Evaluative Research B2B SaaS
Context

Bench is workforce planning for construction

Construction companies use Bench to allocate people across projects. But projects don't appear overnight — there's always a period where a company has bid on work but hasn't won it yet.

These pre-award projects are called pursuits. Bench had no concept of them.

Not being able to track pursuits had led to lost sales opportunities with potential customers.

Project Gantt — before pursuits
The Problem

Users were hacking the product to fill the gap

These workarounds created allocation conflicts and dirty data — but they were a clear signal that demand was real.

1% Allocations

Allocating people at 1% to mark potential work without affecting real forecasts

Colour Coding

Using project colours as a status system outside the intended workflow

Shell Projects

Empty projects with zero allocations and notes indicating who could fill roles

17% Signal

17% of accounts were already tracking multiple fields related to pursuit stage — quantified demand

Research Process

Understanding the full picture before designing

"People wanted to track pursuits so they could build forecasts to predict how best to allocate their workforce in the coming months and years."
Cross-functional Alignment

Involving all squads early

Running HMW with product, design, and dev helped surface where everyone thought pursuits were headed — and which areas mattered most to each team.

HMW brainstorm — cross-squad session
Remote research sessions (Covid era)
Technical Challenge

The concept worked. The backend didn't exist.

The features clients were most excited about couldn't be built — Bench only had date-dependent statuses, which created hard constraints on what a "pursuit" could be.

Current State

Status tied to project start/end date. No user-controlled status. Allocation conflicts when hacking pursuits in.

Proposed Architecture

New "project type" concept + date-independent statuses — enables pursuits today and custom statuses as a future unlock.

After deep discussion with dev and PM, we agreed the backend lift was worth it. Getting this foundation right would pay dividends well beyond pursuits.

Final Design

Pursuits across every surface

Pursuits impacted every part of the product where project status appeared. Hatched border + reduced opacity creates visual differentiation without disrupting existing patterns.
